- lanna: https://lannacheck-ops.github.io/cart263/project-1/

The second project (lannacheck-ops) leaned more into playful interaction.

This one felt less linear and more exploratory, allowing me to click around and discover elements on my own. What stood out here was the responsiveness. Things reacted immediately to my input, which made the experience feel engaging and alive. From a code standpoint, this suggests strong use of event listeners and possibly DOM manipulation to dynamically change elements

I found it compelling how small interactions, like hovering or clicking, could completely change the mood of the interface. One surprising aspect was how intuitive it felt without needing instructions, which shows thoughtful design decisions behind the scenes. Overall the design and layout of the site is curated nicely it sets and mood and tone for the site. I also really enjoy the animation that happens with the character.

- Syla: https://browneyegirl.github.io/explore/about.html

The third project (browneyegirl) focused heavily on visual exploration using google API to find abandoned buildings. It felt more like a digital environment than a structured experience. I was particularly drawn to the layering of images and how movement or navigation revealed new elements. It created a sense of depth that made the project feel immersive. Technically, this might involve positioning, 3D rendering, and possibly animations or scroll-based effects. What I found most interesting was how the visuals carried the experience more than explicit interaction. the project invited me to explore at my own pace without pressure. I really like the fact that they had a about page, giving more information and letting the user navigating themselves throughout the website. The touch of having a spray can and a canvas where we can draw was a nice way to wrap up the site.

Across all projects, what surprised me most was how each student used (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) but achieved completely different outcomes. One emphasized narrative and timing, another interaction and responsiveness, and the third visual immersion and atmosphere
